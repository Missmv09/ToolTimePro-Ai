Termination Checklist

Employee separation process for California employers

CALIFORNIA FINAL PAY REQUIREMENT

Involuntary Termination: Final wages must be paid IMMEDIATELY at time of termination.
Voluntary Resignation (72+ hours notice): Final wages due on last day of work.
Voluntary Resignation (less than 72 hours notice): Final wages due within 72 hours.

Failure to pay on time = Waiting time penalties of one day's wages for each day late (up to 30 days).

Pre-Termination (If Applicable)

  • Document reason for termination
    Keep written record of performance issues, policy violations, or business reasons
  • Review personnel file
    Ensure documentation supports decision; check for any protected class concerns
  • Prepare final paycheck
    Include all wages, overtime, commissions, bonuses owed, and accrued PTO (required in CA)

Final Pay Calculation

  • Regular wages through last day
    Calculate hours worked including partial day
  • Overtime wages owed
    Include any overtime from final pay period
  • Accrued, unused PTO/vacation payout
    REQUIRED in California - cannot be forfeited ("use it or lose it" policies are illegal)
  • Commissions and bonuses
    Include any earned but unpaid amounts
  • Expense reimbursements
    Any outstanding business expenses
Deduction Warning

California law severely limits what employers can deduct from final pay. You generally CANNOT deduct for: damaged equipment, cash shortages, uniforms, or tools - even if employee signed an agreement. Consult an attorney before making any deductions.

Equipment & Property Return

  • Company vehicle and keys
    Document mileage and condition
  • Tools and equipment
    Check against equipment checkout list from hire date
  • Uniforms and safety gear
    Note: Cannot deduct cost from final pay if not returned
  • Building keys / access cards
    Collect all keys and deactivate access codes
  • Company phone/tablet
    If applicable

System Access Termination

  • Disable ToolTime Pro app access
    Remove from worker app immediately
  • Remove from scheduling system
    Cancel any upcoming job assignments
  • Remove from team communication
    Group chats, email lists, etc.
  • Change any shared passwords
    Building codes, equipment locks, etc.

Required Notices & Documentation

  • Provide COBRA notice (if applicable)
    Required within 14 days if employer has 20+ employees with health plan
  • Provide Cal-COBRA notice (if applicable)
    For employers with 2-19 employees offering health insurance
  • Provide EDD pamphlets
    DE 2320 (UI benefits) and DE 2515 (Disability)
  • Provide Health Insurance Marketplace notice
    Information about Covered California options
  • Employment verification letter (if requested)
    Dates of employment and job title only - do not disclose reason for termination

Post-Termination

  • Update payroll system
    Mark as terminated, update records
  • Retain personnel file
    Keep for minimum 4 years (some records require longer)
  • Respond to EDD if UI claim filed
    Respond within 10 days of notice
