Form I-9: Employment Eligibility Verification
U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)
REQUIRED Federal

Timeline: Section 1 must be completed by employee on first day. Section 2 must be completed by employer within 3 business days of start date.

Employer Requirements

  • Verify ALL employees regardless of citizenship (must not discriminate)
  • Physically examine original documents (copies not acceptable)
  • Accept any valid List A document OR one List B + one List C document
  • Do NOT specify which documents the employee must provide
  • Retain I-9 for 3 years after hire OR 1 year after termination (whichever is later)
  • Make forms available for inspection by DHS, DOL, or DOJ
Warning: Fines for I-9 violations range from $252 to $2,507 per form for first offenses, and up to $25,076 per form for repeat violations. Knowingly hiring unauthorized workers can result in criminal penalties.
Form W-4: Employee's Withholding Certificate
Internal Revenue Service (IRS)
REQUIRED Federal

Timeline: Must be completed before first paycheck. Employees may update at any time when their situation changes.

Employer Requirements

  • Provide form to all new hires (including rehires)
  • If employee doesn't complete W-4, withhold as "Single" with no adjustments
  • Do NOT advise employees on how to fill out the form
  • Keep W-4s on file - do NOT send to IRS unless requested
  • Begin withholding based on new W-4 by start of first payroll period ending 30+ days after receipt
