⚠
California Employers: Timing Matters!
Many of these documents must be provided on or before the first day of work. Failure to provide required notices can result in penalties of up to $100 per employee per pay period.
1

Before First Day

1-7 days prior
  • Send Offer Letter
    Include position, pay rate, start date, at-will statement, and any contingencies (background check, drug test)
  • Receive Signed Offer Letter
    Keep signed copy in employee file
  • Run Background Check (if applicable)
    Must follow FCRA and California "ban the box" requirements
    Obtain separate written authorization before running check
  • Set Up Workspace & Equipment
    Desk, tools, vehicle, phone, uniforms, safety gear, keys, parking pass
  • Add to Payroll System
    Set up in ToolTime Pro or your payroll provider
  • Schedule Orientation
    Block time on first day for paperwork, training, and introductions
2

First Day — Required Forms

Day 1
📋
Forms marked REQUIRED must be completed on Day 1
Keep all signed forms in the employee's personnel file for at least 4 years after termination.
  • Form I-9 (Employment Eligibility) Required
    Section 1 must be completed on or before first day. Section 2 within 3 business days.
    Verify documents in person. Do not request specific documents.
  • Form W-4 (Federal Tax Withholding) Required
    Employee completes to determine federal income tax withholding
  • Form DE-4 (California Tax Withholding) Required CA
    California state income tax withholding certificate
  • Wage Theft Prevention Notice (DLSE Notice) Required CA
    Labor Code 2810.5 requires this notice with pay rate, pay period, employer info, and more
    Must be provided at time of hire AND whenever information changes
  • Sexual Harassment Prevention Pamphlet (DFEH-185) Required CA
    California law requires distribution to all employees
  • Rights of Victims of Domestic Violence Notice Required CA
    Must inform employees of rights under Labor Code 230 and 230.1
  • Paid Family Leave Pamphlet (DE 2511) Required CA
    Information about California's Paid Family Leave program
  • State Disability Insurance Pamphlet (DE 2515) Required CA
    Information about California's State Disability Insurance
  • Workers' Compensation Pamphlet Required CA
    Time of Hire Pamphlet explaining workers' comp rights
  • Direct Deposit Authorization (Optional)
    If employee wants direct deposit, collect bank info and signed authorization
  • Emergency Contact Form
    Collect emergency contact information for safety purposes
3

First Day — Company Policies

Day 1
  • Employee Handbook Acknowledgment
    Provide handbook and have employee sign acknowledgment form
    Include at-will statement in acknowledgment
  • Meal & Rest Break Policy
    Review California meal (30 min) and rest break (10 min) requirements
  • Timekeeping Procedures
    Explain how to clock in/out, record time, and report hours worked
  • Paid Sick Leave Policy
    Explain accrual rate, usage, and carryover (minimum 40 hours/year in CA)
  • Safety Training
    Job-specific safety orientation, PPE requirements, emergency procedures
  • Anti-Harassment Policy
    Review policy and reporting procedures
  • Drug-Free Workplace Policy (if applicable)
    Review policy and have employee acknowledge
4

Within First 30 Days

Days 1-30
  • Report New Hire to EDD Required CA
    File DE 34 within 20 days of start date (can file online at e-Services)
  • Complete Section 2 of I-9
    Must be completed within 3 business days of start date
  • Enroll in Benefits (if applicable)
    Health insurance, 401(k), other benefits per eligibility requirements
  • Complete Sexual Harassment Training CA
    Required within 6 months for non-supervisors, 30 days for supervisors
    Employers with 5+ employees must provide training
  • Job-Specific Training
    Complete any role-specific training requirements
  • 30-Day Check-In
    Informal review to address questions, concerns, and early performance
5

Ongoing Requirements

Ongoing
  • Maintain Updated I-9
    Re-verify work authorization before expiration if applicable
  • Provide Pay Rate Change Notices
    New Wage Theft Prevention Notice required whenever pay changes
  • Annual Sexual Harassment Training (Supervisors)
    2-hour training every 2 years for supervisory employees
  • Document Performance Issues
    Maintain records of any disciplinary actions or performance concerns
