üìã California's ABC Test

Under AB5, workers are presumed to be employees unless ALL three factors are met. Failing any single factor means the worker must be classified as an employee.

A

Free from Control

The worker is free from the control and direction of the hiring entity in connection with the performance of the work, both under the contract and in fact.

B

Outside Usual Course

The worker performs work that is outside the usual course of the hiring entity's business.

C

Independent Business

The worker is customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business of the same nature as the work performed.

‚ö†Ô∏è California Labor Law Penalties

Non-compliance with California labor laws can result in significant penalties. Understanding these risks helps you stay compliant.

Waiting Time Penalty
Late payment of final wages. Penalty is one day's wages for each day late, up to 30 days.
Up to 30 days wages
Meal Break Violation
Failing to provide a 30-minute meal break for shifts over 5 hours. One hour of pay per violation.
$15-$25+/day per worker
Rest Break Violation
Failing to provide 10-minute rest breaks for every 4 hours worked. One hour of pay per violation.
$15-$25+/day per worker
Misclassification Penalty
Willful misclassification of employees as independent contractors.
$5,000 - $25,000 per violation
Unpaid Overtime
Failing to pay overtime at 1.5x (or 2x for 12+ hours). Includes back pay plus penalties.
Back pay + liquidated damages
Pay Stub Violations
Missing or incorrect information on pay stubs.
$50-$100 per pay period
